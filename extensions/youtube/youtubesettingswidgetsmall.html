Getting your youtube cookie
Normally YouTube cookies get refreshed/updated while you are on Youtube. This means that if you use a normal cookie you will need to keep updating StreamRoller. To avoid this either use another browser or an incognito window, login to youtube, get the cookie as explained below then close the browser. As long as you don't go back to youtube in that browser the cookie should not get updated.
  1. In a browser, sign into YouTube (either incognito or a browser you don't use for YouTube), then hit F12/ctrl-shift-i/right-click-menu to bring up the Developer Tools/Inspector.
  2. Choose the Network tab.
  3. Select XHR filter.
  4. Enter 'browse' in the Filter box.
  5. Now scroll down the YouTube web page until an entry starting with 'browse' appears. Select it.
  6. Scroll down in the Headers tab until you see Cookie under Request Headers.
  7. Right-click on the value of Cookie and select Copy Value.
